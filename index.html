Communication Strategies

Connecting the Dentist, the Patient, and the Team

Before the Patient Enters

Effective communication starts early. This phase sets the tone for the entire appointment.

1. Preparation & Environment

  • Check details: Know the correct patient file and procedure.
  • The Room: A clean surgery communicates safety without words.
  • Calm Yourself: If you are stressed, the patient will feel it.

2. The Meet and Greet

  • Smile warmly (your eyes smile even behind a mask).
  • Use their name clearly and politely.
  • Guide them to the chair—don't just walk off.
Annie's Tip: Communication is 55% facial expression. Be aware of your face—if you look worried or rushed, the patient will feel unsafe.

The "Secret Language"

You often cannot speak freely during a procedure. DAs and Dentists use non-verbal cues. Click to reveal:

The Leg Tap ▼
Because you sit close together, the dentist might gently tap your leg. It's a discreet way to get attention without the patient noticing.
"Can I help with the lip?" ▼
A polite 'trigger' code to warn the dentist they are pressing on a patient's numb lip, allowing them to adjust without alarm.
Code Words ▼
Specific words for emergencies. E.g., asking for an "X-ray" might actually mean "Check the front desk" or "The patient looks unwell."
"Time for a break" ▼
If there is a safety breach (e.g., broken instrument), use this phrase to stop the procedure immediately so the issue can be fixed.
Eye Contact ▼
Watching the dentist's eyes helps you anticipate their next move. It also conveys honesty and readiness.

After Treatment & Teamwork

Communication defines the culture of the practice. Being a good team player involves actions, not just words.

The Unwritten Rules

  • Restock immediately: Don't leave an empty box for a colleague in a rush.
  • Status Updates: If you can't finish a task, tell your team: "I'm just leaving that there."
Feedback - The "No Blame" Approach:
Issues like incorrect PPE use should be discussed in team meetings without blame. Focus on how the team can improve, rather than punishing an individual.
